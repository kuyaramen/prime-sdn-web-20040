## Writing Style

### Core Philosophy

PRIME SDN's writing style is editorial, sophisticated, and inspiring. We write like a premium travel magazine—evocative, descriptive, and emotionally engaging.

### Guiding Principles

1. **Show, Don't Just Tell** — Use vivid descriptions and imagery
2. **Be Concise** — Every word must earn its place
3. **Evoke Emotion** — Connect with readers on an emotional level
4. **Be Authentic** — Genuine representation of Surigao del Norte
5. **Respect the Reader** — Assume intelligence and curiosity
6. **Maintain Quality** — Professional, polished prose

### Voice Characteristics

- **Sophisticated but Accessible** — Elevated language without being academic
- **Warm but Professional** — Friendly without being informal
- **Inspiring but Grounded** — Aspirational without being unrealistic
- **Confident but Humble** — Self-assured without being arrogant

## Editorial Style

### Magazine-Quality Writing

Our content should feel like it belongs in a premium travel publication:

**Example:**
```
Instead of: "Surigao del Norte has beautiful beaches."
Write: "Pristine white sands meet crystal-clear waters along Surigao del Norte's coastline, where gentle waves lap against shores untouched by time."
```

### Narrative Flow

Content should flow naturally:

1. **Hook** — Emotional or visual opening
2. **Context** — Provide necessary background
3. **Detail** — Specific, vivid descriptions
4. **Connection** — Relate to reader's experience
5. **Inspiration** — End on an uplifting note

### Sensory Language

Engage multiple senses:

- **Visual:** "Golden sunlight," "emerald waters," "towering cliffs"
- **Auditory:** "Rhythmic waves," "distant laughter," "gentle breeze"
- **Tactile:** "Warm sand," "cool ocean spray," "rough stone"
- **Emotional:** "Peaceful," "exhilarating," "awe-inspiring"

## Headlines

### Purpose

Headlines capture attention, set tone, and guide expectations.

### Characteristics

- **Concise:** 3-8 words maximum
- **Impactful:** Strong verbs and vivid imagery
- **Clear:** Immediately understandable
- **Emotional:** Evokes feeling or aspiration
- **Active:** Present tense, active voice

### Structure

**Hero Headlines:**
- Large, bold typography
- 2-3 lines maximum
- Cinematic and inspiring

**Section Headlines:**
- Clear and descriptive
- 1-2 lines
- Editorial style

**Card Headlines:**
- Short and punchy
- 1 line
- Descriptive

### Examples

**Good:**
- "Discover Paradise in the Philippines"
- "Where Innovation Meets Tradition"
- "Journey Through Surigao del Norte"

**Avoid:**
- "Welcome to Our Website"
- "About Us Page"
- "Services We Offer"

### Capitalization

- **Title Case:** Major words capitalized
- **All Caps:** Section labels only (small font)
- **Sentence Case:** Never for headlines

## Descriptions

### Purpose

Descriptions provide context, detail, and emotional connection.

### Length Rules

**Hero Descriptions:**
- 40-50 words maximum
- 2-3 sentences
- Evocative and concise

**Section Descriptions:**
- 30-60 words
- 2-4 sentences
- Informative and engaging

**Card Descriptions:**
- 15-25 words
- 1-2 sentences
- Descriptive and brief

### Writing Guidelines

1. **Start Strong** — Engaging opening sentence
2. **Be Specific** — Concrete details over generalities
3. **Use Active Voice** — "Surigao del Norte offers" not "is offered by"
4. **Avoid Jargon** — Clear, accessible language
5. **End with Purpose** — Why this matters

### Examples

**Hero Description (45 words):**
"PRIME SDN is the official promotional and digital platform of Surigao del Norte, showcasing the province's natural beauty, tourism experiences, cultural heritage, innovation ecosystem, investment opportunities, and sustainable future."

**Section Description (40 words):**
"From world-class surfing at Cloud 9 to the serene waters of Sugba Lagoon, Surigao del Norte offers adventures that awaken the spirit and create memories that last a lifetime."

**Card Description (20 words):**
"Pristine white sands meet crystal-clear waters in this hidden paradise, perfect for swimming and relaxation."

## Button Text

### Purpose

Button text guides user action with clarity and confidence.

### Characteristics

- **Action-Oriented:** Starts with verb
- **Clear:** Unambiguous meaning
- **Concise:** 2-4 words maximum
- **Inviting:** Welcoming tone
- **Specific:** Describes the action

### Primary Buttons

**Examples:**
- "Explore Surigao"
- "Discover More"
- "Get Started"
- "View Activities"

**Avoid:**
- "Submit"
- "Click Here"
- "Continue"
- Generic labels

### Secondary Buttons

**Examples:**
- "Learn More"
- "View Details"
- "Read Story"
- "See All"

### Text Link CTAs

**Examples:**
- "Explore Surigao →"
- "Learn More →"
- "Discover →"
- "View All →"

**Note:** Arrow indicates direction and action.

## Section Labels

### Purpose

Section labels provide context and hierarchy.

### Characteristics

- **Uppercase:** All caps for consistency
- **Small Font:** 12-14px
- **Wide Letter Spacing:** 0.2-0.25em
- **Brand Blue Color:** #3B82F6
- **Concise:** 2-4 words

### Examples

**Good:**
- "ABOUT PRIME SDN"
- "FEATURED DESTINATIONS"
- "INNOVATION HIGHLIGHTS"
- "UPCOMING EVENTS"

**Avoid:**
- "Section 1"
- "Introduction"
- "More Info"
- Generic labels

### Placement

- 8-12px above heading
- Consistent spacing
- Left-aligned
- Never centered

## Storytelling

### Narrative Structure

Every piece of content should tell a story:

1. **Introduction** — Set the scene
2. **Development** — Build understanding
3. **Climax** — Peak interest or emotion
4. **Resolution** — Inspire action or reflection

### Story Elements

**Setting:**
- Describe the place vividly
- Establish context
- Create atmosphere

**Characters:**
- People (locals, visitors, innovators)
- Communities
- Stakeholders

**Conflict/Challenge:**
- What problem was solved?
- What opportunity was seized?
- What transformation occurred?

**Resolution:**
- Positive outcome
- Impact on community
- Future implications

### Storytelling Techniques

**Show, Don't Tell:**
```
Instead of: "The beach is beautiful."
Write: "Golden sands stretch toward the horizon, where the ocean meets the sky in a brilliant display of blues."
```

**Use Specific Details:**
```
Instead of: "Many tourists visit."
Write: "Travelers from around the world flock to Cloud 9 to experience its legendary surf breaks."
```

**Create Emotional Connection:**
```
Instead of: "This is a good place to visit."
Write: "Surigao del Norte welcomes you with open arms, offering experiences that touch the soul and create lasting memories."
```

## Content Hierarchy

### Visual Hierarchy

1. **Hero Heading** — Largest, most prominent
2. **Section Heading** — Large, bold
3. **Card Heading** — Medium, bold
4. **Body Text** — Standard size
5. **Captions/Labels** — Small, subtle

### Information Hierarchy

1. **Primary Message** — One main point per section
2. **Supporting Context** — Brief explanation
3. **Details** — Specific information
4. **Action** — What to do next

### Scanning Pattern

Users scan content in this order:

1. **Headings** — Determine relevance
2. **Images** — Visual interest
3. **Descriptions** — Get details
4. **CTAs** — Take action

Design content to support this pattern.

## Reading Level

### Target Audience

Our content should be accessible to:

- **General Public** — Clear, understandable language
- **Tourists** — Engaging, descriptive
- **Investors** — Professional, credible
- **Researchers** — Accurate, detailed

### Reading Level Guidelines

- **Target:** 8th-10th grade reading level
- **Sentence Length:** 15-20 words average
- **Paragraph Length:** 2-4 sentences
- **Vocabulary:** Accessible, not overly technical

### Simplification Techniques

1. **Use Simple Words** — "Use" instead of "utilize"
2. **Shorten Sentences** — Break long sentences
3. **Avoid Jargon** — Explain technical terms
4. **Active Voice** — Clearer and more direct
5. **Concrete Examples** — Illustrate abstract concepts

## Length Rules

### Maximum Lengths

**Hero Description:**
- 40-50 words
- 2-3 sentences

**Section Description:**
- 30-60 words
- 2-4 sentences

**Card Description:**
- 15-25 words
- 1-2 sentences

**Paragraphs:**
- 3-5 sentences
- 50-80 words

**Headlines:**
- 3-8 words
- 1-2 lines

### Minimum Lengths

**Hero Description:**
- 30 words minimum
- 2 sentences minimum

**Section Description:**
- 20 words minimum
- 1 sentence minimum

**Card Description:**
- 10 words minimum
- 1 sentence minimum

### Editing for Length

1. **Remove Filler Words** — "very," "really," "quite"
2. **Combine Sentences** — When appropriate
3. **Cut Redundancy** — Say it once
4. **Focus on Impact** — Every word must matter
5. **Test Readability** — Read aloud

## SEO Principles

- Siargao
- Innovation ecosystem

**Secondary Keywords:**
- Cloud 9
- Sugba Lagoon
- Sohoton Cove
- Startup ecosystem
- Investment opportunities

### SEO Best Practices

**Headings:**
- Include primary keywords
- Keep natural and readable
- Don't keyword stuff

**Descriptions:**
- Naturally incorporate keywords
- Focus on user value
- Maintain editorial quality

**Meta Descriptions:**
- 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword
- Compelling and actionable

**Alt Text:**
- Descriptive and specific
- Include relevant keywords
- Help accessibility

### Content Quality

SEO should never compromise content quality:

- **Write for humans first, search engines second**
- **Provide genuine value**
- **Be authentic and accurate**
- **Maintain editorial standards**
